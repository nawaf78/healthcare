Founded 2004

Why we built Cedar.

Cedar Medical Center was founded by Dr. Elena Vasquez and a small group of community physicians who shared a frustration: the nearest hospital was forty minutes away, and their patients — many of them elderly, many without cars — were making health decisions based on distance, not medical need.

They raised the funds, hired the team, and opened a 40-bed facility in 2004. Within three years the community response was overwhelming — demand outpaced capacity and a major expansion followed in 2007, bringing Cedar to its current 120-bed, eight-department form.

Today we treat 30,000 patients per year and employ more than 200 healthcare workers — most of whom live in this same community. We are not just a hospital. We are neighbours, caring for neighbours.

What We Believe

Our Values

These principles are not written on a wall. They are practised in every ward, every consultation room, and every interaction with a patient or family member.

Dignity in Every Encounter

We treat every patient — regardless of their background, condition, or circumstances — with full dignity and respect. Healthcare should never feel dehumanising.

Clinical Excellence, Always

We hold every clinician to the highest standard — not because we have to, but because we believe our patients deserve nothing less. Continuous education and peer review are core to how we operate.

Transparency with Patients

Patients deserve to understand their diagnosis, their options, and the risks involved — in clear, honest language. Informed patients make better decisions. We support that always.

Community Responsibility

We are part of this community, not just a service within it. That means outreach clinics, subsidised care for those who cannot afford it, and investing our resources back into the people around us.
